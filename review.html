My Review of Software Engineering 1A

January 27, 2019


This review is a bit late, since as of now it has already been a couple weeks into second semester! Nevertheless, better late than never (something that I've been saying to myself a lot over the semester).

I'll start off by reviewing the courses that I took this semester:

CS 137: Welcome to introduction to programming in C! We learned about basic data structures as well as some important topics such as PageRank, Big O-Notation, and Linked Lists towards the end. Overall, this course was pretty nice and chill.

MATH 115: This was our linear algebra course. I actually really enjoyed this course! Something to note is that depending on where you went to high school, either a lot of it will be review or it will all be totally brand new.

MATH 135: For a lot of people (including me) this was our first introduction to proofs. I found this course to be very challenging at first, but I slowly got used to it. My advice: even though the assignments are difficult, try to do them by yourselves! I used to spend my entire Saturday every week working on the assignment. It paid off.

MATH 117: Beware! This Calculus course starts off pretty slow, and is mostly review from high school. But it definitely picks up a bit in second semester and let's just say that the exam is something that you really need to study for

ECE 105: This introduction to classical mechanics course was definitely the most challenging course of first semester. I'm not sure if a course like this is really neccesary for Software and ECE students but at least it teaches you how to study!

Overall, I'd say that there definitely was a big jump from high school to university. I used to think that I had great study habits in high school, but I quickly discovered that I don't. The general strategy that I used in high school was to spend 2 days before a test doing nothing but that subject. I realized quickly within my first couple of weeks that
1: I simply didn't have enough time to do this! I had to learn how to consistently study so that I wouldn't be cramming for 2 whole days.
2: The tests and quizzes were generally more difficult and tested on stuff not explicitly taught in class.
To be honest, I still haven't gotten the hang of properly studying, but I did realize that it's a good idea to make sure I understood what each day's lecture was about by the end of the day, or at max: the end of the next day. Memorizing could come later, but general understanding had to be prioritized.

Emotionally, the transition was tough on me. I was not used to doing poorly on quizzes and imposter syndrome hit me hard. For the first month or so, I thought that I was completely alone in this. Everyone else seemed fine. But I soon realized that imposter syndrome affects so many of us, and none of us are completely sure of anything. I also learned that in general, my fellow students love to help others. If I got stuck on something I could always ask my friends for help.

All in all, the transition was more difficult than I had anticipated but I still had a great time in 1A making friends and memories that will last for a lifetime.
